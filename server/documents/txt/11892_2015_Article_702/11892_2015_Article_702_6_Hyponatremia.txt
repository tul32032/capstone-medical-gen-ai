Section: Hyponatremia
---
Most patients with Wolfram syndrome have diabetes mellitus and bladder dysfunction in combination with diabetes insipidus. The dose escalation of desmopressin for the treatment of diabetes insipidus should be carefully done because demopressin may cause hyponatremia. Hyponatremia is a common clinical problem in patients with Wolfram syndrome that requires careful management.

Hypogonadism is seen in some patients. Impaired fertility and erectile dysfunction in male patients and infertility, amenorrhea, and oligomenorrhea in female patients have been reported. These conditions could be treatable and managed in standard way.
